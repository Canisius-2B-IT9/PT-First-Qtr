Effect of Navel Orange Peel and White Mushroom
Compost on Ampalaya Plant

Introduction

Composting is the purposeful decomposition of items such as food scraps, leaves, branches, or any compostable
item to enhance plant growth or production. Our project aims to delve deeper into the science of composting as
an enhancer for plant growth. Composting is valuable, especially to the community because it gives a reliable
and efficient way to grow and produce fruits and vegetables. Our study aims to enhance the already effective
composting method by adding white mushrooms – an ingredient, which can gardeners add to compost piles but is
low in vitamin C and navel orange peel – a common ingredient in compost piles and is notably high in vitamin C.
Both fruits are high in magnesium and calcium, which are other nutrients essential in compost. The partner
community we were assigned to – SAKADAB (Samahan ng May Kapansanan sa Dabaw) can benefit from our study because
we have noticed that many of the communities and institutions in Davao still buy expensive composts to help grow
their plants and crops. Our study aims to help the SAKADAB institution find an alternative way to use compost on their plants.

Problem Statement

How does the navel orange peel and white mushroom compost affect the height of the ampalaya plant
in centimeters after one month?

Hypothesis

If the navel orange peel and white mushroom compost is used, then the ampalaya plant will grow
taller in centimeters after one month.
